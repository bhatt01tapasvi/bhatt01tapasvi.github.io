BIO

In 2023, I graduated from Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS), Pilani with a dual degree in Mechanical Engineering and Master of Science in Chemistry. During my undergraduate studies, I worked under the supervision of Dr. Suvanjan Bhattacharyya on various projects, including entropy and exergy analysis in heat exchangers.
Currently, I am pursuing my Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in the School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore under the guidance of Prof. Eddie. My research focuses on optimizing cold chain logistics for vaccine transport using phase-change materials and incorporating artificial intelligence for better thermal management.
Also, I’m a chameleon, shifting research areas like my mood; from cold chain logistics to PIV image processing with deep learning. What’s next? I also don't know. Stay tuned!

RESEARCH AREAS

1: Cold Chain Logistics using PCM [With NTU]

As part of my PhD research, I’m focused on cold chain logistics, particularly in maintaining vaccine efficacy during transportation using phase-change materials (PCM). I’m leveraging machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) for predictive modeling and optimization, while incorporating IoT for real-time monitoring. Additionally, I'm exploring the use of mycelium bacteria for thermal insulation to enhance sustainability.

2: Heat Transfer [With BITS Pilani]

My work focuses on thermodynamic analysis—specifically energetic and entropic (exergetic) assessments—of heat transfer processes, primarily in heat exchangers. These studies have key applications in electronics cooling and solar panel cooling, as well as other similar systems. I specialize in using active techniques to enhance heat transfer efficiency, including magnetic fields and mechanical vibrations to accelerate the process and improve thermal management.

3: Chameleon [Self]

Welcome to my world of ideas—where projects pop up like mushrooms after rain. I call this research area "Chameleon" because, like the creature itself, I effortlessly switch from one topic to another, often abandoning a few (okay, maybe more than a few) along the way. But every once in a while, I manage to finish something and learn something cool in the process. The best part? I'm always open for collaboration, especially if you've got a quirky idea up your sleeve. Let’s make it happen!
