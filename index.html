About Me

In 2023, I graduated from BITS Pilani with a dual degree in Mechanical Engineering and Master of Science in Chemistry. During my undergraduate studies, I worked under Dr. Suvanjan Bhattacharyya on various projects, including entropy and exergy analysis in heat exchangers.

Currently, I am pursuing my Ph.D. in the School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at NTU Singapore under Prof. Eddie. My research focuses on optimizing cold chain logistics for vaccine transport using phase-change materials and AI for thermal management.

Also, I'm a chameleon, shifting research areas like my mood; from cold chain logistics to PIV image processing with deep learning. What's next? I also don't know. Stay tuned!

Research Areas

Cold Chain Logistics using PCM [With NTU]

As part of my PhD research, I'm focused on cold chain logistics, particularly in maintaining vaccine efficacy during transportation using phase-change materials (PCM). I'm leveraging ML and AI for predictive modeling and optimization, while incorporating IoT for real-time monitoring.

Heat Transfer [With BITS Pilani]

My work focuses on thermodynamic analysis of heat transfer processes, primarily in heat exchangers. These studies have key applications in electronics cooling and solar panel cooling. I specialize in using active techniques to enhance heat transfer efficiency.

Chameleon [Self]

Welcome to my world of ideas—where projects pop up like mushrooms after rain. I call this research area "Chameleon" because I effortlessly switch between topics. The best part? I'm always open for collaboration!
